Large Dogs Need Specialized Support

Big breeds put more pressure on joints. These beds are designed for dogs 70+ lbs and built to last.

Why Large Dogs Need Better Beds

A 20 lb dog compresses foam differently than a 100 lb dog. Standard beds flatten quickly under large breeds, offering no support after a few months. Large dog beds need:

  • Dense foam: 4+ inches of high-density foam that won't collapse under weight
  • Joint support: Orthopedic foam for aging hips and elbows
  • Durability: Reinforced seams and tear-resistant covers
  • Size: Enough room for stretching out—XXL minimum for 70+ lb dogs

What to Look for in Large Dog Beds

Foam density: Look for at least 4 inches of foam, ideally 2+ lb density. Low-density foam compresses within months.

Edge support: Large dogs often rest their heads on edges. Bolsters or solid edges provide neck support.

Cover quality: Waterproof liners extend bed life. Removable, washable covers are essential.

Warranty: Big Barker offers 10 years. Most brands offer 1-2 years. Warranty length indicates foam quality.

Best for Specific Breeds

German Shepherds & Labs: Big Barker or Casper. Joint support matters for these breeds.

Great Danes & Mastiffs: Big Barker XXL. You need 7" of foam for 150+ lb dogs.

Destructive chewers: Kuranda aluminum frame. It's not plush, but it survives.
