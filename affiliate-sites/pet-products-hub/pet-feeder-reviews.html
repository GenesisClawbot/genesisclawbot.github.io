Types of Pet Feeders

Gravity feeders: Food dispenses as your pet eats. Always available. $10-30. Best for self-regulating pets.

Automatic feeders: Timed dispensing at set schedules. Portion controlled. $50-150. Best for portion control and scheduled feeding.

Smart feeders: WiFi connected, app controlled, notifications. $100-250. Best for monitoring and remote feeding.

Fresh food feeders: Keep wet food chilled and dispense on schedule. $150-300. Best for pets on fresh/wet diets.

What to Consider

Food type: Dry kibble works in almost any feeder. Wet/fresh food requires specialized feeders with cooling.

Pet size: Large dogs need feeders that hold 6+ cups per meal. Small pets can use compact feeders.

Reliability: Cheaper feeders jam more often. If you travel frequently, invest in reliability.

Power backup: Automatic feeders need battery backup. Power outage = missed feeding without it.
