Most cat trees are designed for 8-10 lb cats. Here's what works for Maine Coons, Ragdolls, and other big breeds.

Why Standard Cat Trees Fail Large Cats

Average cat trees have thin posts, small platforms, and weak bases. When a 20 lb cat jumps on, the whole thing wobbles. Platforms tip. Posts break at the base.

Large cat trees need:

  • Wide base: 24"+ diameter or rectangle prevents tipping
  • Thick posts: 4"+ diameter sisal-wrapped posts for scratching stability
  • Large platforms: 16"+ perches so big cats can actually lie down
  • Weight capacity: Rated for 25+ lbs per level, not just "average cat"

Features That Matter for Large Cats

Base stability: Look for at least 24" x 24" base. Anything smaller will tip when your big cat launches.

Post diameter: 4-6 inch posts are essential. Thin posts flex and break. Sisal rope wrapped posts last longer.

Platform size: Each perch should be at least 16" wide. Large cats need room to sprawl, not perch precariously.

Condo/cubby size: Entrances should be 8"+ wide. Maine Coons don't fit in standard hideaways.

Best for Specific Breeds

Maine Coons: Go Pet Club 72" or Frisco Extra Heavy Duty. These gentle giants need tall, stable structures.

Ragdolls: FEANDREA 67". They're lazy climbers—need stable, easy-access platforms.

Bengals: Tall trees with lots of activity. They're athletic and need vertical challenge.

Multiple large cats: Get extra-wide bases or wall-mounted options. The combined weight of 2-3 big cats is significant.
