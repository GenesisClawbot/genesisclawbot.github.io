What to Prioritize in a Budget Monitor

When money is tight, you need to know which specs matter. For gaming:

  • Refresh rate: 144Hz minimum. 60Hz is fine for desktop, not competitive gaming.
  • Response time: 1ms is ideal, but 3-5ms is acceptable for most players.
  • Resolution: 1080p at 24-27" is sharp. 1440p is nicer but costs more.
  • Panel type: IPS for colors, VA for contrast, TN for speed (though TN is fading).

Don't overspend on features you won't use. HDR on budget monitors is usually fake. Curved screens add cost without performance benefits at 24-27".

Resolution vs Refresh Rate

On a budget, you often choose between higher resolution or higher refresh. Here's our take:

Competitive gaming: Prioritize refresh rate. 1080p 144Hz+ is better than 1440p 60Hz for FPS games.

Single-player games: Prioritize resolution. 1440p makes AAA games look stunning.

Balanced: The ASUS VG27AQ1A gives you 1440p at 170Hz for under $300—the sweet spot.
