Five years ago, cheap headsets meant tinny audio and uncomfortable ear cups. Today, brands like HyperX and Logitech have brought premium features to sub-$100 price points. You get detachable mics, memory foam, and even surround sound in this range.

What You Get (And Don't Get) Under $100

You get: Good audio quality, decent microphones, comfortable designs, and reliable builds. Most have 2-year warranties.

You don't get: ANC (active noise cancellation), premium materials like aluminum frames, advanced EQ software, or the absolute best directional audio for competitive play.

For most gamers—casual and semi-competitive—these tradeoffs are worth the $100+ savings over premium headsets.

Our Top Pick

HyperX Cloud III wins for build quality, comfort, and sound. It's the safest bet and lasts for years. If wireless is non-negotiable, Logitech G435 delivers surprising value at $79.

On a strict budget? Razer Kraken X at $49 is genuinely good for the price—just expect some comfort compromises.
