Pro tip: Start with mouse + keyboard + headset (under £100). Add a chair when you can. The mousepad is the last thing to upgrade but the cheapest — and it makes a bigger difference than you think.

Upgrade Path: When to Spend More

Mouse first: Once you know your grip style and what weight you prefer, upgrade to a premium mouse like the Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 or Razer DeathAdder V3 Pro.

Keyboard second: If you type a lot, a SteelSeries Apex Pro or Razer Huntsman V3 Pro with optical switches is a massive upgrade in feel.

Headset third: The HyperX Cloud III or SteelSeries Arctis Nova Pro are worthwhile upgrades when you want wireless freedom and better audio.

What NOT to Buy Cheap

Monitors: We don't recommend buying a budget monitor. A bad display affects everything. If you must buy cheap, go for a refurbished or second-hand gaming monitor rather than a new cheap one.

Power supplies: If you're building a PC, never cheap out on the PSU. The rest of your system depends on it.
