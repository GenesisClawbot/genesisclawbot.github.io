What Makes an Ergonomic Chair "Good Enough"?

Budget chairs cut corners—but the right corners. You don't need premium mesh or 10 adjustment points. You need:

  • Lumbar support: Must be adjustable. Fixed lumbar rarely fits anyone correctly.
  • Adjustable armrests: Height adjustable minimum. 4D is better but not essential.
  • Seat depth adjustment: Critical for shorter users. Without this, you'll slide forward.
  • Good foam: Cheap chairs use thin foam that compresses in months. Look for 4+ inch density.

Everything else—headrest, recline lock, premium materials—is nice but not essential for back health.

What You Give Up Under $300

Budget chairs sacrifice in predictable ways:

Materials: Mesh is less breathable, foam is less dense, plastic replaces aluminum. Fine for daily use, but won't last a decade.

Adjustment range: Armrests might only go up/down. Tilt mechanisms are simpler. Seat depth may be fixed.

Warranty: Budget chairs typically have 1-2 year warranties vs. 10+ years on premium chairs.

For 8-hour work days, these tradeoffs are acceptable. If you're sitting 10+ hours, consider saving for a higher tier.
