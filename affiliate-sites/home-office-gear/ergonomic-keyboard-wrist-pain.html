Wrist Pain Doesn't Have to Be Permanent

The right keyboard can reduce strain, correct posture, and help you type pain-free again.

Why Standard Keyboards Cause Pain

Traditional keyboards force your hands into unnatural positions:

  • Ulnar deviation: Wrists bent outward to reach outer keys
  • Pronation: Forearms rotated flat against the desk
  • Wrist extension: Hands bent upward to reach keys

These positions compress nerves and tendons. Over time, this leads to RSI, carpal tunnel, and chronic pain. Ergonomic keyboards address these issues directly.

Types of Ergonomic Keyboards

Split keyboards: Separate left and right halves allow shoulder-width hand placement. Reduces ulnar deviation. Microsoft Sculpt and Logitech Ergo are examples.

Tented keyboards: Keys tilt upward, reducing forearm pronation. The angle varies—10-20 degrees is common. Steeper angles offer more relief but take longer to adjust.

Contoured keyboards: Key wells cradle your fingers. Kinesis Advantage2 is the extreme version. Longest learning curve but best long-term results.

Compact keyboards: Removing the numpad lets you keep your mouse closer, reducing shoulder strain. Works well if you rarely need 10-key.

What to Expect When Switching

Week 1-2: Your typing speed will drop 30-50%. Your hands are learning new positions. This is normal.

Week 3-4: Speed returns. Pain should decrease. If pain persists, check your desk height and chair.

Month 2+: Full speed restored, pain significantly reduced. Many users can't go back to regular keyboards.
