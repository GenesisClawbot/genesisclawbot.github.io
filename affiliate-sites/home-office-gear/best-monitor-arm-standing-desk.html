Standing Desks Need Special Monitor Arms

When your desk moves up and down, your monitor arm needs to stay steady. Here's what works.

Why Monitor Arms Matter More for Standing Desks

Standing desks change height by 20+ inches. A fixed monitor position means poor ergonomics at one height or both. The right monitor arm keeps your screen at eye level regardless of standing or sitting.

Cheap arms wobble when extended. Standing desks amplify this problem. You need arms designed for the full range of motion.

What to Look For

  • Weight capacity: Add 2-3 lbs to your monitor weight. You want margin for stability.
  • Reach: Standing desks are deeper. Arms need 18"+ horizontal reach.
  • Vertical range: Must adjust 20+ inches to cover desk height changes.
  • Cable management: Built-in routing keeps cables from catching during transitions.
  • Mount type: Clamp mounts work best with standing desks. Grommet mounts for thick tops.

Dual Monitor Considerations

Dual monitors on standing desks need either two single arms or one dual arm. For most setups, two single arms offer more flexibility—you can position monitors independently.

Dual arms work well if both monitors are the same size and you want them side by side. Avoid dual arms with mismatched monitor sizes.
