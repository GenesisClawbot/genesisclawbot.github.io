#1 Logitech G305 Lightspeed — Best Gaming Mouse

⭐ The legendary budget wireless mouse. Still unbeaten after all these years.

At around £35-45, the G305 is the best wireless gaming mouse you can buy at any price point. Hero sensor, 250hr battery, lightweight at 99g. It embarrasses mice 3x the price.

Yes, it's not ambidextrous (it's right-handed). But if you want the best wireless experience for FPS gaming on a budget, this is it. Many pro gamers still use this as their travel mouse.

Sensor: Hero 12K (equivalent to 12000 DPI)
Battery: 250 hours (AA battery)

#2 Redragon K552 RGB — Best Budget Keyboard

⭐ The keyboard that started the budget mechanical revolution. Still king.

Around £25-35, this is the gateway drug to mechanical keyboards. Outemu switches (clicky or linear options), compact TKL layout, RGB backlighting. It's not fancy, but it works.

Build quality is obviously plastic-heavy compared to £100+ boards. But for the price? It's remarkably solid. Many gamers started here and never looked back.

Switch: Outemu Red/Blue (hot-swappable)
Layout: TKL (no numpad)

#3 HyperX Cloud Stinger Core — Best Budget Headset

⭐ Surprisingly good audio and mic for the price. Comfortable too.

At £25-35, the Cloud Stinger Core punches well above its weight. Comfortable memory foam ear cups, decent noise-cancelling mic, acceptable audio quality for gaming.

It's wired (no wireless option at this price), and the audio isn't going to satisfy audiophiles. But for competitive gaming? The mic quality is better than many £80 headsets we've tested.

Type: Wired (3.5mm)
Mic: Noise-cancelling, flexible

#4 Razer DeathAdder V3 Lite — Best Ergonomic Mouse

⭐ Great ergo shape without the premium price tag.

Around £35-45, the DeathAdder V3 Lite brings Razer's legendary ergo shape to the budget category. 6 programmable buttons, solid sensor, comfortable for long sessions.

It's wired (no wireless), but at this price that's expected. The ergo shape is genuinely comfortable if you have larger hands and use a palm grip.
