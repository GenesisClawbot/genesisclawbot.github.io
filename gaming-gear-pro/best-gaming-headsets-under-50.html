Wired vs Wireless Under £50

True wireless gaming headsets under £50 are rare — most wireless options at this price have latency issues or poor battery life. Stick with wired for competitive gaming where every millisecond counts. The HyperX Cloud Stinger Core and Razer Kraken X both have near-zero latency.

If you must have wireless (for console gaming, for example), the Logitech G G335 is a solid option at around £50 — but for pure gaming performance, wired wins at this price point.

7.1 Surround Sound: Does It Matter?

Most budget headsets claim "7.1 surround sound" — but in practice, this is usually virtual surround processed by software. It can help with spatial awareness in games like Apex Legends or Call of Duty, but it's not a substitute for a quality stereo signal.

Our recommendation: don't pay extra for "7.1" branding. Focus on driver size (50mm is ideal) and frequency response instead.
