# The 7 Questions Every Home Seller Wishes They'd Asked Their Estate Agent

Most homeowners spend more time researching a new phone than they do choosing an estate agent. That's a mistake that costs them tens of thousands of pounds.

When you're selling a property worth £300,000 or £3 million, a 1% difference in achieved sale price is £3,000 to £30,000. The agent's fee is a rounding error compared to what a skilled negotiator can deliver — or lose.

Here are the seven questions you should be asking before you sign anything.

---

## 1. "What is your negotiation strategy for my property?"

Anyone can stick a "sold" sign in the window. Not everyone can hold firm on price when a buyer comes in low.

Ask specifically: *How will you handle a below-asking-price offer?* The right agent will have a clear script, will know the seller's bottom line without being told, and will have a track record of closing gaps between offer and asking price.

Red flag: Any agent who immediately says "we'll get you the best price" without asking about your property, your timeline, or your circumstances.

---

## 2. "What is your local market knowledge — specifically for my street and property type?"

Agents are generalists by necessity. But within that, some genuinely know a street better than anyone — they know which houses sold for what, which developments have cladding issues, which roads have planning applications pending.

Ask: *What properties on [your road] have sold in the last 12 months, and how did your strategy differ for each?*

This question separates the agents who work your street from the ones who just list on Rightmove and wait.

---

## 3. "What will you do in the first two weeks that a budget online agent won't?"

Most sellers choose agents based on fees because they don't know what to look for. The first two weeks of a listing are critical — that's when most of the viewings happen and when your property is freshest to buyers.

Ask specifically: *What marketing will you do in week one?* Professional photography? Video walkthrough? Drone footage? Brochures? Feature in the agent's email newsletter to active buyers?

The agents worth hiring are proactive. They have buyers already queuing for properties like yours.

---

## 4. "How do you qualify buyers before scheduling viewings?"

There is nothing worse than clearing your Saturday morning for six viewings, only to discover two of the buyers are "just browsing" and one is an investor who wants to halve your price.

Good agents qualify buyers before they book. They'll check mortgage agreements in principle, confirm chain status, and verify motivation. Ask your agent what their qualification process looks like.

---

## 5. "What is your fall-through rate — and why?"

Not every accepted offer becomes a completed sale. Chains collapse, mortgages fail, surveys uncover issues. The question is how often this happens and how quickly the agent reacts when it does.

A 30% fall-through rate is a warning sign. Ask: *What happened with those sales, and what would you do differently if it happened on my property?*

---

## 6. "Will I work with you directly, or will my file be passed to a junior?"

This matters more than most sellers realise. Many high-street agencies rotate instructions between staff to manage volume. You might sign with the branch manager and end up being handled by someone three months into the job.

Ask: *Who will I speak to day-to-day? Can I have their direct number?*

The agent worth hiring will put their own mobile number in your contacts.

---

## 7. "What happens if my property doesn't sell within the first four weeks?"

If an agent has no answer to this, they weren't planning to do anything differently. The right agent will already have a week-by-week plan: price review, marketing refresh, buyer feedback analysis, strategy adjustment.

Ask: *At what point do we discuss price? What data will you show me to support that conversation?*

---

## The Bottom Line

The estate agent you choose will either leave money on the table or protect the value you've built in your home. These seven questions won't guarantee you pick the right one — but they'll immediately expose the wrong ones.

Spend 20 minutes asking these questions. It could be the most valuable conversation you have all year.
