# Why Your AC Runs Fine But Your Energy Bill Keeps Spiking

Your air conditioner is working. You can feel the cool air. The house is comfortable. But when your energy bill arrives, it looks like you left the oven on for a week.

You're not imagining it. And you're not alone.

Summer energy spikes are one of the most common calls we get — homeowners confused about why their bills have jumped £80-£150 when their behaviour hasn't changed. Here's what's actually happening.

---

## The Obvious Suspects

Before we get into the sneaky ones, let's rule out the obvious:

**Thermostat creep.** You set it to 24°C in spring. By July, it's on 20°C and you don't remember changing it. Each degree lower adds about 3-5% to your energy use.

**Running the fan constantly.** Ceiling fans and portable fans consume energy even when they're not doing much. If no one's in the room, they're waste.

**Old, inefficient units.** If your AC is over 10 years old, it could be operating at 50-60% efficiency — working twice as hard for the same result.

Those are real. But there's a whole other layer of culprits most homeowners miss.

---

## The Sneaky Energy Drains

### 1. Dirty Filters

This is the #1 cause of high summer bills we see in homes we service. A clogged air filter forces your AC to work dramatically harder. Dirty filters can increase energy consumption by 5-15%.

The fix: Check your filter monthly during summer. If it's visibly grey, replace it. Most residential filters cost £8-£20 and take 60 seconds to change.

### 2. Refrigerant Levels (It's Not Just About the Freon)

Low refrigerant doesn't just cool poorly — it causes the compressor to run constantly, which is where the real energy drain happens. Your AC can feel like it's working fine while the compressor burns through electricity at maximum rate.

Signs of low refrigerant: takes longer to cool down, hissing or bubbling sounds, ice on the copper lines.

### 3. Blocked Condenser Unit

The outdoor unit needs airflow to expel heat. If it's buried in leaves, overgrown plants, or has debris caked between the fins, it can't breathe — and your efficiency drops fast.

Quick check: stand 2 feet away from the unit. You should feel warm air exhausting. If the air feels barely warm, the unit is struggling.

### 4. Duct Leaks and Disconnects

Up to 30% of cooled air can be lost through leaks, disconnects, or poorly insulated ducts — especially in lofts and crawlspaces. Your AC works hard, but the cool air goes nowhere useful.

You won't see this without a thermal camera inspection. We offer free duct efficiency assessments for homeowners in [Service Area].

### 5. The "Sleep Mode" Myth

Many people leave their AC on eco or sleep mode all day thinking it saves energy. In a well-insulated home on a moderate summer day, this can work. But in a typical British semi with poor insulation and south-facing rooms, leaving the AC off all day means the walls, furniture, and mattress absorb heat all morning. When you come home and crank it to 18°C, the system runs flat out for 2-3 hours to catch up — using more energy than if you'd maintained a steady temperature.
