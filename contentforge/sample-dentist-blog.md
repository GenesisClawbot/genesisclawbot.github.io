# Invisalign vs. Braces: Which Actually Works Better for Adults?

You're not a teenager. Your teeth have had decades to settle into their crooked ways. And you're wondering: is it too late for me?

Short answer: No. Adults make up the fastest-growing segment of orthodontic patients in the UK, with the British Orthodontic Society reporting a 75% increase in adult treatment requests since 2019.

But which option is actually better for you? Let's cut through the marketing.

---

## What Each Treatment Actually Involves

**Traditional braces** use metal or ceramic brackets bonded to each tooth, connected by a wire that your orthodontist adjusts every 4-6 weeks. Treatment typically lasts 18-36 months.

**Invisalign** uses a series of clear, removable plastic aligners custom-made from a 3D scan of your teeth. You switch to a new set every 1-2 weeks, gradually moving teeth into position. Most adult cases take 12-18 months.

Both work. The differences matter.

---

## The Honest Comparison

### Aesthetics

Invisalign wins by a mile. The aligners are nearly invisible — most patients say colleagues and friends don't notice them. Ceramic braces are less visible than metal but still noticeable. If image matters to you in your professional or social life, this is a significant factor.

### Comfort

This one surprises people: both can be uncomfortable. Braces cause irritation from brackets and wires. Invisalign causes pressure sensation when you switch aligners (which fades within 24-48 hours). Many adults report that Invisalign feels more manageable because there's nothing fixed to irritate the inside of your mouth.

### Discipline Required

Here's where adults need to be honest with themselves. Invisalign requires wearing the aligners for 20-22 hours per day — taking them out only to eat, drink anything other than water, and brush. If you travel frequently, have a demanding job, or are simply forgetful, this becomes a real challenge.

Fixed braces do the work whether you're thinking about them or not. For patients we know won't comply with aligner wear, we always recommend braces.

### Treatment Speed

For straightforward cases, Invisalign is often faster. For complex cases — severe crowding, significant bite issues, rotation of back teeth — traditional braces give us more control and can actually achieve the result more efficiently.

### Cost

Both are comparable in the UK private market. Invisalign typically ranges from £2,500 to £5,500 depending on complexity. Traditional braces range from £2,000 to £4,500. The gap has narrowed considerably in recent years.

---

## So Which Should You Choose?

There's no universal answer. It depends on:

- **Your bite**: Complex bite issues often require fixed braces
- **Your compliance**: Can you wear aligners 22 hours a day without fail?
- **Your lifestyle**: Do you play contact sports? Do you give lots of presentations?
- **Your timeline**: How quickly do you want treatment complete?

## What Your Dentist Will Tell You

At your consultation, we'll take photographs, X-rays, and a 3D scan. From that, we can model what each treatment would look like for your specific teeth — including the likely outcome and timeline.

Some patients are surprised to learn they're not a candidate for one option or the other. Some discover their case is simple enough that either would work.

**The most important step is the consultation, not the decision.** Most practices offer a free initial assessment.
