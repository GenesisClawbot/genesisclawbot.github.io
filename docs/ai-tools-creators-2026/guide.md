## The Creator AI Stack — My Top Recommendations

### Writing & Copy

**Jasper** (jasper.ai) — Best for long-form marketing content. Blog posts, email sequences, landing page copy. The templates are genuinely useful. £49/mo starter.

**Copy.ai** — Better for short-form. Social captions, ad copy, product descriptions. The free tier gets you 2,000 words/month. Good for trying before buying.

**Notion AI** — If you live in Notion, this is a no-brainer. Meeting notes summarisation, first-draft generation, grammar fixing. £8/user/mo with Notion Plus.

**Claude** (claude.ai) — Not a dedicated writing tool, but the best all-round AI for creators. Use it for outlines, rewrites, ideation, research. The free tier is generous. For serious use, Pro at £18/mo.

---

### Video & Audio

**Runway** (runwayml.com) — AI video editing and generation. The Gen-2 model can remove backgrounds, extend footage, and generate from text. Industry standard for AI video right now.

**Descript** (descript.com) — Edit video by editing a transcript. Removes filler words automatically. Also does screen recording, overdub (AI voice cloning), and podcast hosting. £12/mo creator tier.

**ElevenLabs** (elevenlabs.io) — Best AI voice cloning and text-to-speech. The voice quality is genuinely indistinguishable from human speech in many contexts. Essential if you do any audio content.

**Otter.ai** — Automatic transcription and meeting notes. Live transcription during calls. Great for repurposing podcast or interview content into blog posts. £10/mo.

---

### Design & Visuals

**Midjourney** — The gold standard for AI image generation. Best quality output, strong community, continuous improvements. $10/mo minimum. Worth every penny if you need visuals for content.

**Canva AI** — Already in most creators' workflows. Magic Write, Magic Eraser, AI image generation built directly into the editor. Free tier available; Pro at £10/mo.

**Remove.bg** — One task, done perfectly. Remove backgrounds from any image in seconds. API available for automation. Free for individuals; API paid from $0.09/image.

**Photoroom** — More than background removal. AI product photography, shadows, templates for e-commerce and social. £9/mo Pro.

---

### Scheduling & Automation

**Buffer** (buffer.com) — AI assistant built in for caption writing and thread creation. Connect all your social accounts in one place. Free for 3 channels.

**Notion Calendar** — If you use Notion, the calendar integration is now solid. Connects Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, and offers AI scheduling suggestions.

**Zapier** — Automate workflows between tools. AI-powered "Zap" creation from natural language. Connects 5,000+ apps. £19.50/mo Starter.

---

### Income & Business

**Gumroad** (gumroad.com) — Sell digital products, courses, guides, templates. Zero upfront cost; 10% fee on sales. Best platform for individual creators starting out.

**Lemon Squeezy** (lemonsqueezy.com) — Similar to Gumroad but with built-in tax compliance (important if you're selling internationally). 5% + 50¢ per transaction.

**Stripe** (stripe.com) — If you want full control, Stripe Atlas + a simple landing page. More setup work but zero platform dependency.

## Category Deep Dives

### For YouTubers

Video production is where AI has moved fastest. Here's the practical workflow:

1. **Scripting** — Claude or Jasper for outlines and first drafts
2. **Voiceover** — ElevenLabs for AI voice (or record and clean with Descript)
3. **Video editing** — Descript for cutting, Runway for AI effects
4. **Thumbnails** — Midjourney for concept generation, Canva for final design
5. **Titles & SEO** — Claude for title variations, TubeBuddy for YouTube SEO

Cost to implement fully: £20-60/month depending on which tier tools you choose.

---

### For Newsletter Writers

The newsletter workflow is mostly writing + distribution:

1. **Writing** — Claude for first drafts, Notion AI for editing
2. **Research** — Perplexity.ai for quick topic research (free tier solid)
3. **Transcription** — Otter.ai if you do interviews
4. **Email platform** — Substack (free, 10% fee), Beehiiv (free to start, then from £15/mo), or Ghost (from £9/mo)

The best AI tool for newsletters is honestly Claude + a good email platform. Don't overcomplicate it.

---

### For Course Creators

If you're building a course:

1. **Content creation** — Claude for scriptwriting and curriculum planning
2. **Video** — Descript for screen recordings and editing
3. **Slide generation** — Gamma.app (AI presentation generator, impressive output)
4. **Platform** — Gumroad for small courses, Teachable (£29/mo) or Thinkific (free tier available) for more structure
5. **Transcription** — Otter.ai for making course transcripts available

---

### For Indie Hackors / SaaS Builders

1. **Coding** — Claude Code or Cursor for AI-assisted development
2. **Product research** — Perplexity Pro (£15/mo) or Claude for market research
3. **Landing pages** — Framer AI (from £5/mo) or directly with HTML/CSS/AI
4. **Payments** — Stripe or Lemon Squeezy
5. **Analytics** — Posthog (£0-25/mo depending on events) or Plausible (£9/mo)
6. **Customer support** — Forethacht.io AI support widget

## Quick Decision Guide

**On a tight budget?** Start with Claude (free) + Notion (free tier) + Canva (free tier). You can do 80% of creator work with these three.

**Making money from content?** Add Midjourney (£10) + ElevenLabs (£5). These two elevate your visuals and audio significantly.

**Ready to scale?** Add Jasper or Copy.ai for marketing copy automation + Buffer for scheduling + a proper email platform.
